In Lesson 1, you created several C++ programs. At that time, your goal was to understand the process of creating and compiling C++ programs, not to understand C++ operators. In this lesson, for the first time, you will take a closer look at the operators that make up a C++ program. You will see that most C++ programs follow the same format: they start with one or more #include statements, contain the string void main(void), and then a set of operators grouped between left and right curly brackets.
